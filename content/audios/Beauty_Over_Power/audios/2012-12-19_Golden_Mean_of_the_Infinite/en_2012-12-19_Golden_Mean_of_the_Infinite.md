The sādhus, they’re interested in the eternal world, the permanent world. This advaya-jnan, this one thing to be known, can be understood in terms of three aspects: Brahmā, Paramātmā 
and Bhagavān. Brahmin, impersonal conceptions, appear superficially to be able to contain everything. Paramātmā, at the other extreme, smaller than the smallest. If these are the extremes of the Infinite, what will be the central conception of the Infinite? The central conception of the infinite means the Golden Mean is Kṛṣṇa conception, which is distinguished by what?
