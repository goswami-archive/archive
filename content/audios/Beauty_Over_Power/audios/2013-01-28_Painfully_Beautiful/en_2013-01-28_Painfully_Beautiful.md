Guru Mahārāj will say in regard to Mahāprabhu, cupids are fainting 
to see the beauty. Sometimes we use the expression. In it's common usage, we'll say someone is painfully beautiful. You look at them and they're so beautiful that it goes that it's some kind of pleasant pain we experience. You can't stop looking at them, you look away from them and you want to look back, but if you look too long they're just painfully beautiful. That's the type of beauty.
