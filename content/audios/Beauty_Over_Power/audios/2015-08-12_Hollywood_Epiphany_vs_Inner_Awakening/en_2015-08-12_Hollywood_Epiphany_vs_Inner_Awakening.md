HOLLYWOOD EPIPHANY VS. INNER AWAKENING
Everyone would like some sort of like a Hollywood epiphany: like the twig breaks, then you become enlightened. But our concept even of enlightenment is—it’s not a static state, it’s not stagnant. So we are capable of growing.
If the finite is going to meet the Infinite, come in connection with the Infinite, at what point is it stagnant? At what point does the finite imbibe or contain the whole of the Infinite? That’s not tenable. But as much as of what Kṛṣṇa wants to reveal to us—that much we will know about ourselves, about Him, about the Supreme Reality.
Our strategy, the devotional strategy is to take shelter of a superior devotee through association and service. And that’s considered a safe position. But you heard recently I used this expression quoting Śrīla Guru Maharāj about the inner awakenment of a disciple. So according to inner awakenment, not only how we conceive guru—how we conceive our self, our notion of self, our conception of self. But that is going to require the dissolution of the layers covering to allow the actual form to manifest.
Śrīla Guru Maharāj has also given an interesting example in this regard about sandalwood and sandalwood pulp. The sevak or the archak who’s doing the service to the Deity, they take a stone, pour some rose water on the stone, then they take a bar of sandalwood and start rubbing that against the grinding stone. And what happens—the sandalwood stick is transformed into its essence, and that fragrance, sandalwood fragrance comes out, and it’s in this paste form. But what you’ll see, happens in time: the sandalwood stick starts diminishing. The more it’s turning into its essential fragrance, it’s dissipating. So through apparent self-dissipation, by engaging ourselves in service, dedicating what is available to us at present, our acquired energy in service, the external conception of self starts dissipating, and the outcome is the essence, the real self, the servitor self—swarup.
