7 Jun 2017.

Q (Pavan Krishna Prabhu): We've heard that pure devotion to the Lord has no motivation, it's ahaituki, with no self-interest. How can we obtain such devotion, if our aspiration is not so pure and we are driven by selfish desires?
How to get rid of these impurities in our heart? Is it possible to motivate someone in such a way that he started to hanker for such devotion? Or does it fully depend on mercy? And what is this pure devotion itself?

- The word ‘motivation’ can be misleading.
- There should be no ulterior motive, no anticipation for the personal gain or reward.
- Krishna is so compelling that automatically [one is] drawn toward Him.
- Service to Krishna is realized in the service to guru and vaishnava.
- Mukunda-preshtha. — Who is most dear to Krishna?
- Guru should be seen directly as Krishna.
- Without the mercy of Nityananda it is not possible to come in connection with Radha and Krishna.
- The Glories of Raghunath Das Goswami.
- Huge delightful festival held by Raghunath Das Goswami for Nityananda Prabhu and His associates.
- Mahaprabhu gives His famous instructions to Raghunath Das Goswami: “In internal world always worship Radha and Krishna.”
- What Krishna doesn't know?
- Ramananda-samvad.
- Lalita-Vishakha.
- To see Bhagavatam and Bhagavad-gita through Sri Chaitanya Charitamrita — that is Gaudia-darshan.
- Mahaprabhu gives His Govardhan-shila and gunja-mala to Raghunath Das Goswami.
- Raghunath Das Goswami prays to Govardhan to give him the shelter.
- Chandravali's opposition group.
- Why are you living: to serve or to exploit?
- "Without You, Radharani, Krishna is scary."
- Deep and pure service aspiration in the heart of Saraswati Thakur.
