S10E70 • With What Mood to Do Service?

Q: With what mood to do seva?
There are times when we have sinned a lot. And when the realization comes, we feel a lot of regrets. Krishna says that He can save us from the reactions of so many sins if we surrender fully. But, the thing is, after we can situate ourselves as the most fallen souls, we are no longer interested in being saved from the reactions of those sins. In some way, it is one reason for us to give the best of ourselves in seva, to do our best to please Krishna, but still, it is not easy to come to terms that we could have sinned so much. (It is like the kind of feeling we have when we hurt our father and after realizing it, we will ask for forgiveness and do our best to please him all the time. But it is not until he has forgiven us that we can get rid of this guilt from our heart and strengthen our bond with him). After the realization of our sins, if we do seva with the mood of enjoying the transcendental bliss that we get while doing it, even then I do not feel that it is okay. So, what should we do in this case? With what mood to do seva? And if we do seva for the act of purifying ourselves, then also, it is like doing it for our own selfish motive, so, eventually, with what mood to do seva?
