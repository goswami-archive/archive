S11E55 • Knowledge is Poison?

00:00:00 Introduction

00:01:56 Shraddha gives us the company of the Lord; by believing in this one thing everything is achieved—like unified field theory; Brahman—paramatma—Bhagavan: three aspects of One; Brahman-based theories accommodate everything

00:07:30 Bhagavan – Krishna in the company of His Shakti; Guru is to be seen like Krishna directly: Krishna-priya, Mukunda-preshtha; impartial look; Ramananda-samvad: whatever rasa one tastes, he thinks it’s the best

00:12:59 According to the devotion in the heart of the devotee – Krishna reciprocates; “different Krishnas”; Ratha Yatra: why Nityananda doesn’t come close to Mahaprabhu; Radharani: no one can extract more quantity and quality of rasa from Krishna, She is Krishna’s equal; Paramatma and Bhagavan—what’s the ratio?—Sun and sunlight in your room

00:17:44 How can Krishna reciprocate with everyone; woman in this world can play different roles; Krishna’s the ultimate multitasking Absolute with an infinite number of recipients; no immigration quota in the spiritual world; Vedas – katha-sagar; when Mahaprabhu says something, it has extra value; are we supposed to extract from the Vedas ourselves? – Six Goswamis did that; Guru Maharaj is shastra-nipun; extracting from the scriptures is a qualification for Acharya

00:24:16 Mahaprabhu’s example: sunrise can be perceived at every moment: that’s true about Krishna-lila and that’s how Krishna reciprocates all these relations simultaneously

00:26:25 Shift to shakti; the proof of approaching Everest is when you start coming in connection with subordinate peaks: Radharani and Her servitors

00:28:09 Sri Upadeshamritam: to reach the last shloka, start with controlling your tongue; Radha Kunda is not conventional water: bathe in the waters of self-sacrifice; according to one’s inner necessity and sincere hankering for truth, one will come in the connection with a certain level of theistic conception and subsequent association; what I like to do in Krishna consciousness; a pool of rasgulas; Krishna is not a sweet ball. Krishna’s happiness is a devotee’s happiness; give Krishna what He wants

00:33:12 8th shloka of Sikshastakam: if what pleases Krishna is heartbreaking to me–that is my happiness; adarshanam; if you think that you’re Krishna conscious – you’re finished

00:38:27 After all this chanting, why doesn’t Krishna reveal Himself to me; resist what comes to you: genuine Krishna consciousness is irresistible; Krishna’s addicted to a particular heart that Krishna cannot resist; causeless and irresistible–in both ways; Daruka is cursing the ecstasy, Radharani curses Her tears; they interrupt Their service

00:41:53 All our innate hankerings are perfectly satisfied—sometimes cannot tolerate so much grace; evaṁ-vrataḥ sva-priya-nāma-kīrtyā; madness for ordinary people; Gaurakishor Das Babaji Maharaj performs his bhajan in the former outhouse – but that was external for him, he couldn’t tolerate the stench of material mentality

00:45:48 Under the garb of scholarship, there can be poison—imitation can be like this; studying Sanskrit and Bengali for the sake of service; are you ready to admit that knowledge is poison? Guru Maharaj comes from a Sanskrit-speaking household; do Gaur and Nityananda know siddhanta when They say that nam-sankirtan is the highest?

00:51:06 Krishna likes to surrender Himself with ignorant people; gyana-shunya-bhaktas: their prem is not obstructed by aishwarya-gyan; sing the glories of Shraddha Devi – that’s how Krishna is revealed. Shridhara-sakala-veti—Sridhar Maharaj knows everything; when knowledge is serving Krishna – then it’s in its rightful position; Saraswati Thakur hammers the topic of gyana-shunya-bhakti: his Gurudev was illiterate, but Saraswati Thakur found all spiritual knowledge in one of the toenails of his lotus feet

00:56:44 Sahajiyas, imitationists in the garb of goodwill, displaying some faith; cautious in selection of association – our real fortune depends there; good company is the most valuable thing; a moment’s association with sadhu can give everything
